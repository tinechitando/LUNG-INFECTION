Know more about Long COVID

The incubation period for COVID-19 is thought to extend to 14 days, with a median time of 4-5 days from exposure to symptoms onset. Overall, an average case of COVID-19 lasts between 1 and 4 weeks. However across the globe, there are patients sustaining the SARS COV-2 infection and are developing wide range of persistent symptoms that do not resolve over the course of many months. According to these patients are being given the diagnosis Long COVID, post-acute COVID-19 syndrome (PACS), or post-acute sequelae of COVID-19 (PASC).

More About this Research

Significance

Reports indicate that approximately 10–20% of COVID-19 patients experience these lingering symptoms.
In the unfortunate event that the model predicts a high percentage of lung infection, then the patients can seek care from their healthcare provider to help manage their symptoms.
The radiological quantification of the severity and progression of lung abnormalities is of great importance in determining the appropriate clinical management and respiratory support for infected patients.

Motivation

The main motivation for this study is the need to raise awareness on post-COVID-19 syndrome and to aid in its diagnosis. The medical and health sectors are seeking advanced technology to help cope with the COVID-19 pandemic. As a ML enthusiast and one eager to help achieve Global Health, this research merges the two for the greater good in health. This research is a contribution to the fight against COVID-19 and its aftermath using Machine Learning.

Limitations

This study will use datasets obtained from the Internet. There is very limited data on post-COVID-19 in the global context. This shortage of data will impact the training of the model, and in turn reduce the accuracy of the predictions. More recently, attention to the problem of the post-COVID-19 syndrome has been increasing, however, there are still many unanswered questions regarding this matter. This consequently impacts the quality of research and understanding of the COVID-19 sequelae
